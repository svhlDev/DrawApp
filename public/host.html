    Graffiti Hierarchy
    1. Tags/Handstyles — Simple, one-color signatures. Most common but can be covered by any higher form.
    2. Throw-ups — Fast, bubble-letter outlines or filled-in names. Designed for speed, meant to cover tags.
    3. Blockbusters — Large, blocky, often silver-and-black letters. Faster than pieces but more complex than throw-ups.
    4. Pieces — Complex, artistic works with multiple colors, shading, 3D effects, and intricate lettering.
    5. Wildstyle/Productions — Highly complex, interlocking lettering or large-scale murals that demand the highest respect.
    Unspoken Rules
    • Respecting Rank — You cannot place a tag over a throw-up, or a throw-up over a piece without causing "beef".
    • Going Over — Covering someone's work is acceptable only if your work is of a higher caliber.
    • Toyness — Beginners ("toys") who skip steps or cover superior work with inferior work are heavily criticized.
